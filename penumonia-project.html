Overview

This project helps to identify pneumonia cases in children, you'll need to categorize some chest X-ray images to determine whether the person has pneumonia or a normal lung. Kindly use the tips given below and pay careful attention to each tip.

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the lungs' air sacs (alveoli), in severe cases, it can lead to death.

Steps

  1. Examine the image thoroughly.
  2. Observe the signs shown in each image carefully, then choose the case accordingly; having pneumoniaor a normallung.
  3. Then choose your confidence level according to your choice and observation.
  4. In case of uncertainty choose unknown.

Rules Tips

In the case of pneumonia, the image may include a few things :

  • Areas of cloudiness/opacity in several concentrated areas or one large area.
  • You may also see a general pattern of opacity that obscures the structure of the lungs, heart, and diaphragm.

In the case of a normal lung, the image may include a few things :

  • Clear lungs without any areas of abnormal cloudiness/opacity
  • There may be structured, web-like vasculature in the lungs but otherwise that area should be clear.
  • In healthy images, you are also more likely to see a diaphragm shadow.

Tips:

  • If the confidence level of your answer is below 50%, then choose unknown. This is the case where you can't define whether it's a pneumonia case or a normal lung.
  • You might choose the unknownoption also if the image is severely cropped, damaged, or taken with a lateral projection where both sides of the lung are not equally visible.
